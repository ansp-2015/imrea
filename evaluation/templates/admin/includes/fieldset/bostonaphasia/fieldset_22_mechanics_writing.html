Instruct the patient to perform the writing tasks listed below

  1. Full name and address
  2. If (1) fails, then type the name and address of the patient on a paper and he/she should copy it
  3. Transcript: The patient should transcribe the sentence printed

Note: The patient should write directly on the page, below the sentence. If the patient can not transcribe in cursive, you must copy it with capital letters.

The writing mechanics assessment is based on verification of the total output of the patient in the written test. Use the five-level scale listed here:

  1. not readable letters
  2. occasional success in single letters (capital letters)
  3. capital letter with some malformed letters
  4. legible cursive writing, but impaired and/or upper and lower case in capital letters
  5. Judged be the same pre-morbid writing, with tolerance for the use of non-dominant hand
